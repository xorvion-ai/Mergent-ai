01 What we collect

We aim to collect as little as possible. Depending on how you use Mergent, that may include:

  • Account data — your email and the sign-in provider you choose (Google, GitHub, magic link).
  • Submitted content — repository URLs, uploaded archives, and API specifications you provide for analysis.
  • Usage data — basic analytics such as which features you use and whether an analysis completed.

02 How we use it

We use your information solely to operate the Service: to authenticate you, run analyses, generate reports, and improve reliability. We do not sell your data.

03 Your code & repositories

We don't keep your source code. Repositories are cloned or extracted into ephemeral, per-session workspaces, parsed for structured facts, and discarded after the analysis run completes.

The parsing step happens server-side. From it we derive a compact set of facts — service names, endpoints, dependency edges, table names, configuration values — which is what powers the rest of the pipeline.

04 AI processing

Mergent uses a large language model (Google Gemini by default) for the reasoning steps of its agents. By design, only the extracted facts — never raw repository dumps — are sent to the model provider.

Model providers process this data under their own terms. We send the minimum necessary to produce your analysis.

05 Cookies & local storage

We use essential cookies and browser local storage to keep you signed in and to remember preferences such as your light/dark theme. We do not use third-party advertising trackers.

06 Data retention

  • Submitted code: deleted when the analysis session ends.
  • Generated reports: retained only if you choose to save them; otherwise per-session.
  • Account data: retained until you delete your account.

07 Your rights

You may request access to, correction of, or deletion of your personal data at any time. To do so, contact us and we'll respond within a reasonable period.

08 Security

We transmit data over encrypted connections and store secrets such as API keys only in environment variables — never in code or repositories. No system is perfectly secure, but we follow sensible engineering practice to protect your data.

09 Changes to this policy

We may update this policy. Material changes will be reflected in the “Last updated” date above.
